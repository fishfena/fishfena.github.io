Ontogenetic bases of craniofacial novelty

The study of the ontogenetic bases of phenotypic novelty is fundamental to understand how evolution and speciation take shape. I use transcriptomic evidence from a nascent adaptive radiation endemic to several hypersaline lakes on San Salvador Island, Bahamas, to test the expression and functionality of adaptive genes on craniofacial divergence and novelty. This unique adaptive radiation consists of 3 pupfishes species with drastically different trophic behaviors and craniofacial morphologies: Cyprinodon variegatus, a generalist or omnivore fish that is also widely distributed across the Caribbean and the North American east coast; and two trophic specialists, a molluscivore, C. brontotheroides, with a unique nasal-protrusion, and a scale-eater, C. desquamator, with longer oral jaws and bigger mandibular muscles.

The olfactory organ as a neuroimmune interface

During my Ph.D. in Kate Whitlock's lab at the University of Valparaiso I described a unique population of innate immune and lymphatic cells in the developing and adult olfactory organs of the zebrafish. Due to the unique location of the olfactory sensory system (constantly exposed to the external environment) we hypothesize that the olfactory rosette has a dual novel function as a immune and sensory organ.

Transgenerational inheritance of host-microbe interactions

Along with Andrea Calixto's lab I have studied the behavioral and genetic responses of the bacterivorous nematode Caenorhabditis elegans to complex host-microbe interactions. We have found that, depending on the nutritional value, as well as the virulence of the bacteria and the amount of time fed on it, transgenerational phenotypic changes can be inhereted to the subsequent generations through small non-coding RNAs from both eukaryotic and prokaryotic origin as a novel form of interkingdom communication.

C. elegans RNA interference

RNA intereference (RNAi) is a form of gene silencing carried out by feeding worms on non-pathogenic bacteria expressing double-stranded RNA matching a worm gene target. Here, RNAi for Dishevelled-2, a polar segment polarity gene involved in canonical Wnt signaling, does not affect the development of the outer labial lateral (green) sensory neurons. #embryo2019

Conservation

Teleost fishes comprise the largest and most diverse group of vertebrates on Earth yet freshwater ecosystems are one of the most highly endangered environments. Generating knowledge about the life history, ecology, and evolutionary adaptations of fish, specially to unique and extreme environments, will benefit the law policies that allows for the protection of some of the still completely undamaged aquatic ecosystems.

There is no Planet B

I believe in reducing all forms of waste: I recycle, reuse, and reduce my consumption of fossil fuel, water, food, and clothes. As a society, we should demand our legislators to ban any economic activity threatening the future of our planet and societal diversity.
