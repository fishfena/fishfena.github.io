M. Fernanda Palominos, Ph.D.

I'm a developmental neurobiologist interested in the adaptation of fish' sensory systems to extreme environments. I was born and raised in the narrowest and longest country on Earth. Now, I'm a Postdoctoral Scholar in Chris H. Martin's lab, in the Museum of Vertebrate Zoology and the Department of Integrative Biology of the University of California, Berkeley.

Ontogenetic bases of craniofacial novelty

The study of the ontogenetic bases of phenotypic novelty is fundamental to understand how evolution and speciation take shape. I use transcriptomic evidence from a nascent adaptive radiation endemic to several hypersaline lakes on San Salvador Island, Bahamas, to test the expression and functionality of adaptive alleles on development. This unique adaptive radiation consists of three sympatric species with drastically different trophic behaviors and craniofacial morphologies: Cyprinodon variegatus, a generalist or omnivore fish that is also widely distributed across the Caribbean and the North American east coast; and two trophic specialists, a molluscivore, C. brontotheroides, that posses a unique nasal-protrusion that is a skeletal expansion of the maxilla, and a scale-eater, C. desquamator, with two-fold longer oral jaws and five-fold bigger adductor mandibulae muscular complex. What is the function of the genes that influence jaw lenght in the adult on jaw development?.

The olfactory organ as a neuroimmune interface

During my Ph.D. in Kate Whitlock's lab at the University of Valparaiso I described a unique population of immune and lymphatic cells in the developing and adult olfactory system of the zebrafish. We suggest that the olfactory organ has a dual olfactory-immune function due to it's unique location, constantly exposed to the external environment.

Transgenerational inheretance of host-microbe interactions

Along with the lab of Andrea Calixto I have used the bacterivorous nematode Caenorhabditis elegans as a model to study host-microbiome interactions. We have found that, depending on the pathogenicity of the bacteria as well as the amount of time fed on it, transgenerational phenotypic changes can be inhereted to the subsequent generations through eukaryotic and prokaryotic small non-coding RNAs as a novel form of interkingdom communication.

C. elegans RNA interference

RNA intereference (RNAi) is a form of gene silencing induced by feeding worms on E. coli expressing double-stranded RNA against a worm gene target. Here, RNAi for Dishevelled-2, a polar segment polarity gene involved in canonical Wnt signaling, does not affect the development of the outer labial lateral (green) sensory neurons.

PNS development in Ascidians

How does peripheral neurons develop in the invertebrate chordate Ciona intestinalis? I used a dominant-negative form of Suppressor of Hairless (SuH), the transducing transcription factor in the Notch pathway, to induce changes in the peripheral nervous system (revealed by Gelsolin:RFP in red). The prominent pigment spots are the otolith and the ocellus.

Commitments

Science communication

I strongly believe that as scientists we are fortunate human beings as we enjoy working questioning the vast beauty of nature. To make positive impacts in society our discoveries must be communicated, specially to the future generations.

Outreach projects

Since 2016 I have participated in Ciencia al Tiro, a Chilean project that seeks to teach kids 'the joy of science'. We develop scientific projects ranging from physics to molecular biology, and have made audiovisual capsules to bring science closest to the people.

Love

I believe that love has no label and that the true value of a person resides within. I try to provide an inclusive, supportive, and welcoming environment everywhere I go.

Our nature

Making mistakes is part of our nature. I believe that we have the opportunity to reflect, correct our behaviours, and do better. I fight each day to stay humble and grateful as I walk this path on the shoulders of the ones that preceded me. I strongly belive that borders between countries are only geographic: as an imigrant in the U.S., and inmigrant-descendent in my own country, I also believe that our nature as humans is to migrate.

Conservation

Teleost fishes comprise the largest and most diverse group of vertebrates yet freshwater ecosystems are one of the most highly endangered environments on Earth. Generating knowledge about the life history, ecology, and evolutionary adaptations of fish, specially to unique and extreme environments, will benefit the formation of laws that protect some of the still undamaged aquatic ecosystems.

There is no Planet B

I believe in reducing all forms of waste: I recycle, reuse, and reduce my consumption on fossil fuel, water, food, and clothes. I believe that as society, we should demand our legislators to ban any type of economic activity that threats the future of our planet and society. I support all forms and efforts against possession of weapons, fascism, colonialism, and oppression.
